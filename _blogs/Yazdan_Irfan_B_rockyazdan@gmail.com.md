You who are reading this right now, might have or will be a Climbing The First Step of there Crucial Ladder in your Life.

 I did mine on 30th of January, 2026. I started my very first Internship(Vinternship) at VLED Labs under Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar and ANNAM.AI under the Guidance of Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar.

Phase - 1 : 

It started with not so high hopes, Watching lectures on ViBe hosted by Nandan Prabhudesai. Keeping my eyes pinned to the screen and my ears to his words. 

So why did I say "Few Crucial Steps"? So as I started to get more involved in the Vinternship, I was given an opportunity to make and maintain a Discord Server for the very Internship I was doing. Of course any one would be excited, but for me it wasn't long as I got banned from the server the moment I joined. It was my own mistakes as I used a very unprofessional UserName. Later I changed it and accepted. As the work began I was getting to talk to and share my thoughts and my knowledge with people and learn from them. I was working with people I never met before, people whom I didn't even know. "So here I came out of my comfort zone and started talking to unknow people".

Learning from ViBe one thing but then learning with and from people was a different experience, ViBe helped me make the Base for the conversation with people who knew about and learn more about it from them. By this time I was hyped, I became a part of a Group who wanted to learn, not from books but from trying to achieve something. 

Then came the Phase of Nomination, we had a task to approach our Senior Co-Intern and request them to Nominate us to Phase - 2 (This is more exciting). In the process of doing this I was tested, thanks to ViBe I knew things. As this was going one I was still working with the team at discord helping interns out and making friends from differnet part of the country.

Phase - 2 :   

Here we had to Complete Case Studies, not just any but real world things. I did some myself, some I got stuck. So for the rescue came the breakout rooms. Co-Interns discussing case studies, I joined and we started to discuss how to do it, finding more ways to understand it as everyone had same but different way of approching the same question. This breakout room became a regular thing, more people started to join. People who were Dual Degree holders, had so much knowledge, I got so much to learn. 

We ended up making a group who join a breakout room regularly and discuss case studies. Now we know each other quite a lot, some live nearby some very far. But this Internship has made me learn that staying in one place will get you nowhere.

Now I are a phase were a few of use have taking part in the Hackathon in the internship. More challenges and more learning. Then I will move to projects and contribute to something which has given me so much.

Life : Up few Steps : 

So "The Steps", from a person who would feel worried talking to anyone is having a group of random people with like mind, this internship taught me, "Just speaking up and talking can make you gain so much more than silence will every get you". There is no age of learning or a boundary to how much you can learn. In life Everyday you can get something so unexpected you can't imagine so as one of the greatest man who inspired me said " Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish" -- Sir Steven Paul Jobs
